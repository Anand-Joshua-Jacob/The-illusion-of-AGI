I. Planning and Design:

  1. Determine Table Dimensions: Decide on the desired length, width, and height of your table. Consider its intended use and the space where it will be located.

  3. Sketch and Cut List: Create a simple sketch of the table with all dimensions. Generate a cut list that specifies the number of pieces needed, their dimensions, and what each piece will be used for (tabletop planks, aprons, legs).

  4. Tools:

    • Square

      • Mortise and Tenon Joints: A more advanced method that provides a stronger, more traditional joint.
      • Pocket Hole Joinery: Using a pocket hole jig, drill angled holes into the apron pieces and use pocket hole screws to attach the legs.
      • Metal Table Leg Brackets: Use commercially available brackets to attach the legs.

  4. Final Touches: Inspect the table for any imperfections and address them. Consider adding felt pads to the bottom of the legs to protect your floor.

V. Important Considerations:
